WHY & HOW

In Andhra Pradesh, several remote villages face significant challenges in providing basic education facilities. These villages often lack adequate infrastructure, teaching staff, and resources, leading to high dropout rates and limited educational attainment. Here are a few remote villages in Andhra Pradesh struggling with access to quality basic education:


1.Manyam District (formerly part of Visakhapatnam Agency area): Many tribal villages in this newly formed district lack proper school infrastructure. The difficult terrain and lack of transportation make it hard for children to reach schools, while teacher shortages further impact education quality.
                                                                                                                                                                                     2.Gudurupadu, Prakasam District: In Prakasam's remote areas, including Gudurupadu, schools are often under-resourced, and there is a scarcity of qualified teachers. These villages struggle to maintain consistent attendance due to poverty and a lack of educational facilities.
                                                                                                                                                                                     3.Varadaiahpalem, Chittoor District: This village in a remote part of Chittoor district faces poor educational infrastructure, especially for higher grades. Many students in this area have limited options for continuing education beyond primary school.
